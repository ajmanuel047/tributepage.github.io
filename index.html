Alistair Cockburn

One of the initiators of the agile movement in software development

A Brief History On Alistair Cockburn

Born in the year November 19, 1953, Alistair Cockburn is a computer programmer and an American citizen who has made great contributions in software developement.

He is globally known as one of the initiators of the agile movement in software development which is a set of practices intended to improve the effectiveness of software development professionals, teams, and organizations

The Career of Alistair Cockburn

He started his career path by studying methods of object oriented software developement.

He would later go on to get a degree in computer science at the Case Western Reserve University and later on received his PhD from the University of Oslo.

Some of His Major Contributions

  • Helped write the Manifesto for Agile Software Development
  • The Declaration of Interdependence which are management principles intended for project managers of software development projects
  • Co-founded the International Consortium for Agile
  • The inventor of the Cockburn Scale for categorizing software projects
  • Created the Heart of Agile concept

Alistair Cockburn's books

He also prides himself as an author and he has written several books a with the most popular being Writing Effective Use Cases which presents practical guide to software developers for writing use cases.

Some of his other books are:

  • Agile Software developement
  • Crystal Clear: A Human-Powered Methodology for Small teams
  • Surviving Object-Oriented Projects

Conclusion

Alistair Cockburn is truly a remarkable individual and his contributions are of great importance in software developement.
